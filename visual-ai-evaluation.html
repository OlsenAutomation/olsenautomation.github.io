Case 01 • CPG reconstruction

Damaged package to credible retail product

Reference fidelity

Assignment

Reconstruct the torn front wrapper as a believable unopened retail package while using the intact visible packaging as the source of truth.

Why the intermediate failed

  • It cleaned and centered the package successfully
  • It still placed nutrition/back-label content on the front face
  • It therefore looked polished while violating the central instruction
  • The corrected result restores coherent front-package structure and a transparent candy window

Constraint-based prompt excerpt

Preserve the logo, red wrapper, clear candy window, strawberry graphics, crimped white ends, glossy plastic, wrinkles, and package proportions. Remove exposed back-label information from the reconstructed front surface unless it belongs on the actual front design.

Evaluator conclusion

A result can be visually polished and still fail if it contradicts the package’s front/back logic. Product truth takes priority over decorative cleanup.

Case 02 • Wearable-tech spec image

Commercial polish without losing product identity

Multi-reference analysis

Evidence hierarchy

  • Composition reference controls layout and campaign feeling
  • Casual product photograph controls geometry and proportions
  • Interior closeup controls sensor shape and inner-band detail

Model risks

Stylizing the ring into a generic luxury object, rounding the block-style sensor, changing the notch, or confusing matte and polished materials.

Evaluation principle

When references disagree in purpose, assign each one authority over a specific category. This prevents the model from treating every image as an interchangeable style reference.

Case 03 • Ecommerce normalization

From casual capture to clean product presentation

Commercial usability

Evaluation focus

Edge integrity, perspective, clean background separation, believable grounding shadow, accurate color, material texture, and preserved product proportions.

Pass condition

The edited result should look intentionally photographed—not pasted onto white, over-smoothed, or rebuilt into a different product.

Consistent scoring

A shared rubric makes comparisons explainable and reduces the temptation to choose whichever output feels more impressive at first glance.

AdherenceDid it follow every material instruction?
FidelityDoes it preserve evidence from the references?
AccuracyAre claims and visible details supportable?
QualityAre lighting, form, texture, and edges credible?
UtilityCould the result serve its stated purpose?
